Upon matriculating at Tufts University in Medford, Massachusetts, I had no idea what I wanted to pursue. I took nearly every "Intro to..." class I could find, including, but not limited to: Economics, Sociology, Psychology, Cognitive & Brain Sceinces, Philosophy, and Computer Science, plus I dabbled in French, and some Calculus. One decision I did make, though, was to walk onto the swim team, which turned out to be one of the greatest experiences of my college career.

A year later, as the deadline to declare a major grew nearer, I finally settled on Cognitive and Brain Sciences (CBS). CBS is technically a branch of the Psychology department, though it has its own inter-departmental steering committee, including professors from the psychology, philosophy, child development, and computer science departments. As someone who had trouble declaring a major at all, I enjoyed the opportunity to take all these interesting classes in different departments across the university. Additionally, this major pushed me to explore Computer Science, something I never would have gravitated towards without this push; the following semester, I decided to minor in CS.
